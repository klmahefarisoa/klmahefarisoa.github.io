About Me

I am a One Health advocate and researcher exploring the connections between science and policy. My work focuses on biodiversity conservation and trying to understand why scientific evidence doesn't always translate into effective policy action.

I'm curious about the gaps between what we know from research and what actually happens in practice. Through my work at KU Leuven, I'm learning how to better bridge these worlds‚Äîworking with communities, policymakers, and fellow researchers to find pathways that connect knowledge with real-world change.

Being a mother while doing this work reminds me daily why it matters. I approach research with both urgency and humility, knowing there's still so much to learn and that meaningful progress requires collaboration across disciplines and perspectives.

The Journey

From forest floors to research halls‚Äîa path shaped by persistence, questions, and a refusal to choose between caring for wildlife and caring for people.

My work began in the forest as a wildlife veterinarian. I could treat injured animals (particularly lemurs, endemic primates threatened with extinction), but I kept watching their habitats disappear while communities struggled alongside the very wildlife I was trying to protect. It became clear these weren't separate problems.

That realization sent me back to school‚Äîtwice‚Äîwhile continuing fieldwork. Two master's degrees later, I was pregnant with my first child when the opportunity came to pursue a PhD at KU Leuven. Moving abroad, starting a doctoral program, becoming a mother‚Äîall at once. People asked if it was too much. But motherhood didn't weaken my resolve; it clarified why this work matters.

Now my work centers on a critical gap: the space between what science tells us and what policy actually does. We have research showing how to protect biodiversity and support communities, but that knowledge often stops at journal publications. Working in an interdisciplinary setting, I'm trying to understand why‚Äîand how we can build better pathways from evidence to action.

Along the way, I co-founded Natiora Defenders, connecting conservation with community needs on the ground. The journey hasn't been straightforward, but the most meaningful paths rarely are.
